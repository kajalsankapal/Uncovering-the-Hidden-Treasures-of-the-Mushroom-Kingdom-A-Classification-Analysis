Mushrooms

A mushroom or toadstool is the fleshy, spore-bearing fruiting body of a fungus, typically produced above ground, on soil, or on its food source. Toadstool generally denotes one poisonous to humans.

Mushroom Categories

Boletus

A genus of fungi that includes some of the most prized culinary mushrooms. They are known for their distinctive appearance and rich flavor.

Lactarius

Also known as milkcap mushrooms, these fungi produce a milky latex when cut or broken. They are found in various woodland habitats.

Russula

A large and diverse genus of mushrooms. Some species are edible while others are toxic, making proper identification crucial.
